TECHSTORE PLUS — SUPPORT ARTICLE
TITLE: Laptop Won't Power On — Diagnosis and Resolution
Article ID: SUP-LPT-001
Effective January 2024
Applies to: All TechStore Plus laptop models (Laptop Pro X1, Laptop Air S2,
            Laptop Max Pro, Laptop Lite V3, WorkStation Z)

================================================================================
SYMPTOM
================================================================================

Pressing the Power button produces no response: no LED activity, no fan spin,
no display output. The laptop appears completely dead, or shows only a brief
LED flash before shutting down again.

================================================================================
SAFETY FIRST
================================================================================

- Do NOT attempt to open the laptop chassis — doing so voids the Standard Warranty.
  If you suspect a hardware fault requiring internal access, use the warranty
  claim process (see support_warranty_claim_process.txt).
- Disconnect all external devices (monitors, USB drives, docking stations) before
  starting. External devices can prevent a normal POST (power-on self-test).

================================================================================
STEP-BY-STEP DIAGNOSIS
================================================================================

STEP 1 — Verify power adapter and outlet
- Connect the original TechStore Plus power adapter (a third-party adapter may
  not supply sufficient wattage). The Laptop Pro X1 and Max Pro require a 65W
  USB-C adapter; Laptop Air S2 and Lite V3 use a 45W USB-C adapter; WorkStation Z
  uses a 130W barrel connector adapter.
- Plug directly into a wall outlet — not a power strip or UPS, which can cause
  voltage irregularities.
- Look for the charging LED indicator:
  * Solid amber: charging (battery was deeply discharged)
  * Solid white: charged and connected
  * No LED: adapter not recognized or port damaged — skip to Step 4.
- If the LED lights up, wait 5 minutes, then attempt to power on.

STEP 2 — Perform a hard reset (EC reset)
This resets the Embedded Controller, which manages power sequencing.
1. Disconnect the power adapter.
2. Press and hold the Power button for 15 seconds. The laptop will not turn on —
   you are draining residual capacitor charge.
3. Reconnect the power adapter.
4. Press the Power button normally (1 second).
This resolves the majority of "dead laptop" reports and should be tried before
any other steps.

STEP 3 — Test with a minimal configuration
1. Remove any USB drives, SD cards, HDMI cables, or docking stations.
2. If the laptop has a removable battery (Laptop Air S2 revision 1 only):
   remove the battery, connect AC power, and try to power on.
3. Attempt to power on. If successful, reconnect peripherals one at a time to
   identify the problematic device.

STEP 4 — Check the charging port
- Inspect the USB-C charging port for debris or bent pins using a flashlight.
- Gently clear debris with a dry toothbrush. Do NOT insert metal objects.
- Try the second USB-C port if available (the Laptop Pro X1 has two USB-C ports).

STEP 5 — Battery deep-discharge recovery
If the battery was fully discharged and left uncharged for an extended period
(over 3 months), it may have entered a protection lockout state.
1. Connect the power adapter.
2. Do NOT press the Power button.
3. Leave the laptop connected for a minimum of 4 hours.
4. After 4 hours, attempt to power on.
This allows the battery management IC to slowly recover the cell voltage above
the lockout threshold.

STEP 6 — BIOS/UEFI recovery (advanced)
If the laptop powers on briefly but shuts off immediately:
1. With the adapter connected, hold Fn + Power for 10 seconds. This forces
   a UEFI recovery on all TechStore Plus laptop models.
2. If a recovery screen appears, follow on-screen instructions to restore the
   default BIOS settings.
3. If no recovery screen appears, proceed to warranty service.

================================================================================
STILL NOT WORKING?
================================================================================

If none of the above steps resolve the issue, the most likely causes are:
- Failed DC power circuit or charging IC (hardware fault — warranty eligible
  if within coverage period)
- Defective battery cell (warranty eligible within 12 months of purchase)
- Motherboard component failure (warranty eligible)

File a warranty claim at techstoreplus.com/claims or call 1-800-TECH-PLUS.
Bring your proof of purchase and serial number. Do NOT open the chassis — any
signs of unauthorized disassembly will be noted and may affect warranty eligibility.
