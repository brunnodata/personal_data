TECHSTORE PLUS — SUPPORT ARTICLE
TITLE: Router NX300 Won't Connect to the Internet
Article ID: SUP-NX300-001
Effective January 2024
Applies to: Router NX300 (TSP-RTR-NX300)

================================================================================
SYMPTOM
================================================================================

The Router NX300's WAN LED is red, amber, or off, and connected devices report
"No internet" even though the Wi-Fi connection to the router appears active.
Your devices can ping 192.168.1.1 (the router's LAN IP) but cannot reach
external addresses.

================================================================================
BEFORE YOU START
================================================================================

Gather this information — you may need it during troubleshooting:
1. Your ISP connection type: Dynamic IP (DHCP), Static IP, or PPPoE.
   (Check your ISP contract or call your ISP if unsure.)
2. PPPoE username and password (if applicable — supplied by your ISP).
3. The router serial number (bottom label, format NX300-XXXXXXXX).

================================================================================
STEP-BY-STEP DIAGNOSTIC
================================================================================

STEP 1 — Check physical connections
- Confirm the Ethernet cable runs from the modem's LAN port to the router's WAN
  port (blue-labeled on the NX300).
- Swap the cable with a known-good cable if available.
- Check for bent or damaged RJ-45 pins on both ends.

STEP 2 — Power-cycle the modem first, then the router
- Unplug the modem's power cable. Wait 30 seconds.
- Plug the modem back in. Wait 60 seconds for the modem to re-establish the ISP
  connection (all LEDs stable on the modem).
- Then power-cycle the router: press the Power button off, wait 10 seconds, then
  press Power on. Wait 60 seconds.
- Check the WAN LED: solid white = connected.

STEP 3 — Verify ISP connection type in the Setup Wizard
- From a device connected to the router's Wi-Fi, open http://192.168.1.1.
- Log in with your admin credentials.
- Navigate to Internet (or WAN) settings.
- Confirm the connection type matches your ISP:
  * Dynamic IP (DHCP): no credentials needed — click "Get IP Automatically."
  * Static IP: enter the IP address, subnet mask, gateway, and DNS provided by
    your ISP.
  * PPPoE: enter the username and password provided by your ISP. These are
    different from your router admin credentials.
- Click Save and wait 30 seconds for the router to reconnect.

STEP 4 — Check for MAC address cloning
Some ISPs register the modem's MAC address. If you recently replaced the modem
with the router:
- In the web UI, go to Internet > MAC Address Clone.
- Enter the MAC address of the old modem (printed on its label), or click
  "Clone Current Device MAC" to use your computer's MAC address.
- Save and restart the router.

STEP 5 — Check DNS settings
- In Internet settings, set Primary DNS to 8.8.8.8 and Secondary DNS to 8.8.4.4.
- Save and test connectivity.

STEP 6 — Factory reset as last resort
If all steps above fail:
- Hold the Reset button on the router's back panel for 10 seconds.
- All LEDs will flash, and the router will reboot to factory defaults.
- Re-run the Setup Wizard (see product manual for steps).
- WARNING: This erases all custom settings, including SSID names and passwords.

================================================================================
STILL NOT RESOLVED?
================================================================================

If the WAN LED remains red after completing all steps, the issue may be:
- A hardware fault in the router (contact TechStore Plus for warranty service)
- An ISP outage (check your ISP's status page or call them)
- A defective cable from the ISP to your modem (ISP's responsibility)
