What NOT to Do in Refining Prompts

1. DO NOT introduce artificial blockers or rigid assertions in hidden testsThe mistake: Writing code-level assertions in test_outputs.py that reject valid in-place engineering repair patterns (e.g., copying a functioning configuration block from a decoy).The reason: This creates False Negatives, which are strictly penalized by the audit layer. Hidden verifiers must remain lightweight and purely outcome-based (checking if the strict byte-level round-trip is functional).

2. DO NOT sabotage or maliciously reroute live production configurationsThe mistake: Adulterating active production profiles or environment manifest chains (like pointing the production path to a broken dummy file) just to bait the model.The reason: Hardening must represent real-world enterprise engineering complexity, not artificial breakage. The true production asset must remain completely valid and transparently discoverable through systematic code-tracing.

3. DO NOT leak explicit symptoms or exact code targets in the instructions (Over-signposting)The mistake: Enumerating exact whitespace edge-cases (e.g., mentioning specific tabulations or consecutive spaces) or naming exact dictionary keys (e.g., "delete the Strip normalizer layer").The reason: Lower-tier agents (Fast models) easily converge on the solution through simple pattern matching and regex-like prompt recognition, bypassing the required environmental discovery burden.

4. DO NOT provide a local verification script that acts as a complete OracleThe mistake: Allowing the bundled workspace verifier tool to test the entire evaluation corpus and print detailed source/decoded diff outputs directly into the terminal log.The reason: Agents will treat the local verifier as an authoritative test suite, using trial-and-error loops until it passes. Shift the diagnostic discovery onto passive runtime validation logs instead.5. DO NOT seed shallow, broken, or unrealistic decoysThe mistake: Creating fake placeholder configuration files that throw syntax errors or lack complex training metadata tables.The reason: Advanced models (Smart models) execute automated workspace searches and hash fingerprint checks. Weak decoys are instantly weeded out by simple elimination, narrowing the search space too fast.
